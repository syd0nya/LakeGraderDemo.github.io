Animal life is a big part of Florida. Florida has more than 1,700 streams and rivers, 7,800 freshwater lakes, over 1,000 springs, and 11 million acres of wetlands. The large freshwaters in Florida host the highest diversity of freshwater animals due to the high gradient of rivers and streams, proximity to the continental landmass, and the presence of karst features such as sinkholes and caves.

In Florida, lakes are a vital ecosystem for animals such as fish, frogs, insects, migratory birds, alligators, cottonmouths, water snakes, and more.

Facts about Animal Life in Florida:

  • There are only 2 species of alligators found worldwide - one in Florida, and the other in China.
  • South Florida is the only place where alligators and crocodiles coexist.
  • Florida Lakes are the home of many beautiful and wonderful bird species.
  • Snakes are present in every Florida freshwater body as well as on land.
