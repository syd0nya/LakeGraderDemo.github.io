Turbidity refers to the cloudiness or haziness of water caused by suspended particles such as sediment, algae, organic matter, or microscopic organisms. It is an essential indicator of water quality in lakes and other water bodies. High turbidity can significantly affect the safety of a lake. Florida has sets its turbidity standard for lakes at 29 NTU's above the natural background.

Phosphorus occurs naturally in the environment and is an essential nutrient that plants and animals need to grow. However, too much phosphorus can impact the amount and types of algae found in a waterbody and may contribute to the development of algal blooms. Algal blooms can detract from the recreational use of water and, in some cases, can result in deoxygenation of deep waters leading to mortality of species such as lake trout.
