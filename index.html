Freshwater Lakes

  Though we often hear references to freshwater, we rarely realize just how integral it is to our lives. Freshwater is responsible for sustaining farming, livestock, and supplying our drinking water. Freshwater also allows the existence of over 125,000 species that depend on clean, fresh water.

  What exactly is freshwater though? The exact definition varies depending on the group, though almost every definition is based on freshwaterâ€™s lack of salt. The most common definition states that freshwater must have a salt content lower than 1%. While this may seem like a small condition to meet, freshwater comprises only 3% of the total water on Earth, and most it is stored in ice caps and glaciers. The remaining freshwater is found in groundwater, swamps, rivers, and lakes.

  While the portion of freshwater found in lakes is relatively small, lakes often become a hub for human interaction. This is especially true in Florida, with over 50 freshwater lakes located across the state. From holidays to a quick weekend break, gathering at a lake is a tried and true tradition of Florida life.

  Despite this, however, many people don't realize all the factors that influence how safe a lake really is. From recent weather, to chemical levels, to the ecosystem that resides in a lake, how "safe" a lake is can often be difficult to pin down. This is especially true in the modern day, as we become more aware of how small changes to the environment can have massive effects on our everyday lives.

Lake Grader

  As you get ready to visit your local lake, check how safe it is using the Lake Grader! This tool provides a quick, easy way for you to determine how safe a lake is. Simply select your desired lake from the drop-down menu and click the button to receive that lake's safety rating.

  Each safety rating is based on a combination of rainfall and turbidity levels in the past few months. To see more details on the lake's safety rating, click on any of the links below the lake's grade. In addition to rainfall and turbidity details, there are also details on wildlife found in Orange County's local lake ecosystems available for reference.
